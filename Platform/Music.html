Notes On Conditional Form

The 1975's Notes on a Conditional Form is a genre-defying album that explores identity, memory, and the human condition. The album is full of cameos, including Greta Thunberg and Cutty Ranks, and is considered the band's most eccentric album.Matty Healy also described the album as a “more intimate, nocturnal and cinematic” record than ABIIOR. During his NME interview, Healy also shared that the album “is very homely, it’s a lot about home, it’s a lot about mental health, it’s a lot about domesticity.”

Notable Songs in the Album

  1. The End (Music for Cars)

    2. “The End (Music for Cars)” is an orchestral re-arrangement of “HNSCC”, deriving from the band’s 2013 Music for Cars EP,The title refers to the bittersweet culmination and conclusion of the Music for Cars, era symbolizing the band’s notable growth and development from their humble beginnings.

  2. The Birthday Party

    3. “There’s a song called “The Birthday Party” that’s just about the interesting social minutiae of house parties. I was gonna do a song that was like, ‘What it was like to be at a house party at 20, 25, and 29’.

  3. Then Because She Goes

    4. “The Because She Goes” is a 90s-reminiscent love track flooded with guitars and dreamy vocals by lead singer Matthew Healy. Matthew sings about having to part with someone he loves- though not permanently- and the sadness that comes with it for the pair of them.

  4. Playing on My Mind

    5. The song stands as one more part of ‘stand-up comedy’ genre in the variety of NOACF musical styles but still stays honest and raises important questions of Healy’s existence, no matter the environment.
